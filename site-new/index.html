Why this exists

01
GTM engineering has no curriculum

RevOps teaches process. Sales enablement teaches decks. Nobody teaches the actual engineering — the embeddings, the signal scrapers, the loops that make outreach intelligent. This does.

02
Open-Brain baked in

Every lesson generates spaced repetition cards. Your review schedule lives in your repo — not an app, not a subscription. Fork it, commit it, own it. The algorithm surfaces the right cards at the right intervals automatically.

03
Terminal-native from zone one

No IDE setup. No extension packs. Claude Code Desktop and the terminal are the permanent environment — not a training phase before something else. Loop engineering is how practitioners build at scale. That's what you learn here.

04
Your repo is the save file

Progress, cards, artifacts — all of it lives in your fork. No accounts. No syncing. Open Claude Code in your mission command folder and pick up exactly where you left off.

05
AI theory with GTM application

Every AI concept is paired with a GTM redirect — where that mechanism lands in signal scoring, outreach generation, account qualification, or pipeline research. Theory and application run in parallel, not in sequence.

06
You build real tools

Signal scrapers. Research handlers. ICP definition engines. Outreach loops. The capstone isn't a demo — it's infrastructure you can drop into a real GTM stack.

07
Outdated by design — not this one

AI moves quarterly. Most courses were written for a market that no longer exists. This curriculum is reviewed against what's shipping, not what shipped.

08
One path. No tab graveyard.

A dozen sources, zero coherence, no mental model. One sequence. One terminal. One place to be.

Open-Brain

Spaced repetition that lives in your repo.

Most courses make you install a flashcard app. Open-Brain runs inside your mission command — every lesson produces cards, the FSRS algorithm schedules your reviews, and everything commits to your fork. Your learning state is yours. No app. No account. No streak to protect.
