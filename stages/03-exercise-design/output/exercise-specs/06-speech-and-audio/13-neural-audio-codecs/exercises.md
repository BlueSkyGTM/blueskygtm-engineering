## Exercises

1. **Implement** a single RVQ forward pass in `signals/examples/rvq_single_step.py`. Create a synthetic continuous latent tensor of shape `[8, 64, 128]` (batch=8, frames=64, dim=128) and a codebook of shape `[512, 128]`. For each frame, compute the nearest centroid via pairwise L2 distance, output the token indices `[8, 64]`, then compute the residual tensor (original latent minus selected centroids). Print the token indices for the first 5 frames and the mean L2 norm of the residuals. Confirm that every residual norm is strictly smaller than the mean distance from the origin of the original latents — this verifies that the quantizer actually reduced error.

2. **Compare** EnCodec, DAC, SNAC, and Mimi on a single 5-second audio clip. Use `torchaudio` to load or synthesize a 24 kHz mono clip. For each codec you can load (via `encodec`, `descript-audio-codec`, or their HuggingFace ports), encode the clip and print: model name, number of codebooks used, frame rate (Hz), total tokens per second, and decode-and-compute MSE against the original waveform. If a model is not installable in your environment, hard-code its published spec values from the lesson and mark it `(spec)`. The terminal output must be a formatted table with all four codecs side by side.

3. **Build** an ablation harness that progressively removes codebooks from an RVQ cascade and measures reconstruction quality. Load EnCodec at 24 kHz with 8 codebooks. For `k` in `[8, 7, 6, 5, 4, 3, 2, 1]`: keep only the first `k` codebooks' tokens, zero out the rest, decode, and compute MSE against the original audio. Print a table with columns `codebooks_used | mse | mse_delta_from_previous`. Verify that the MSE delta grows steeply once you drop below the first 2–3 codebooks, confirming the residual hierarchy. Use at least two audio clips (one speech, one music or tone sweep) and confirm the pattern holds for both.

4. **Trace** the semantic-acoustic split. Load Mimi (Kyutai) and EnCodec on the same 3-second speech clip. Extract codebook-0 token sequences from both. For Mimi, compute the average cosine similarity between its codebook-0 decoder embeddings and the WavLM or DistilHuBERT embeddings of the same audio (use the `transformers` library to get hidden states at matching frame rates). For EnCodec, do the same. Print both cosine similarity values. Confirm that Mimi's codebook 0 has materially higher alignment with the speech-encoder representation than EnCodec's codebook 0. If you cannot install Mimi, substitute with a manual distillation step: fine-tune a small `[256, 128]` codebook on DistilHuBERT hidden states for 500 steps with k-means initialization, then repeat the cosine similarity measurement.

5. **Design** a lead-routing pipeline that mirrors the RVQ residual hierarchy, using real CRM or enrichment data. Pull 50 leads from your Clay table (or a provided CSV at `data/leads_sample.csv` if no Clay access). Treat the first "codebook" as semantic intent: embed each lead's most recent email or LinkedIn message with `sentence-transformers/all-MiniLM-L6-v2`, k-means cluster into 8 groups, and assign the cluster ID as `token_0`. Treat the second "codebook" as firmographic enrichment: query Apollo's API (or the CSV's company-size / industry columns), bucket into discrete bins, and assign `token_1`. Treat the third as behavioral: number of site visits or email opens, bucketed. Build a composite key `(intent_cluster, firmo_bin, behavioral_bin)` for each lead. Write a router in `handlers/lead_rvq_router.py` that assigns each composite key to one of three sales queues (AE, SDR, nurture) based on `token_0` priority (semantic intent dominates) with `token_1` and `token_2` as tie-breakers. Output the full routing table and the distribution across queues. The handler must be importable and callable as `python -m handlers.lead_rvq_router --input data/leads_sample.csv`.

6. **Prove** (or disprove) the claim that token rate is the dominant predictor of reconstruction quality across codec families, independent of architecture. For each of EnCodec (1.5, 3, 6, 12, 24 kbps), DAC (2–16 kbps), and SNAC (0.6–4 kbps), encode and decode 10 diverse audio clips at every available bandwidth setting. Compute MSE and multi-scale STFT spectral loss for each. Produce a scatter plot (token rate on x-axis, MSE on y-axis, color-coded by codec family) saved to `outputs/codec-rate-vs-fidelity.md` as a base64-embedded image alongside a markdown table of the raw data. State in 3–5 sentences whether the data supports or rejects the claim, and identify which codec family is the outlier.
