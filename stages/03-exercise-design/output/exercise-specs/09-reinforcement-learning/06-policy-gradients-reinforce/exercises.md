## Exercises

1. **Implement** a full REINFORCE training loop on `CartPole-v1` from scratch in PyTorch. Define a 2-layer MLP policy (64 hidden units, ReLU, softmax output), collect full episodes, compute Monte Carlo returns with `γ=0.99`, and update the policy using `torch.optim.Adam` with `lr=1e-2`. Print the total reward for every episode. Run for 500 episodes and confirm that the running 50-episode mean reward exceeds 200 by the end.

2. **Compute** and **trace** the per-step quantities for a single episode. Collect one trajectory, then print a table showing, for each timestep: the state, the action taken, the immediate reward, the discounted return `G_t`, the log-probability `log π(a|s)`, and the product `G_t · log π(a|s)`. Verify visually that early actions with high `G_t` receive larger gradient contributions than late actions with `G_t ≈ r_T`.

3. **Extend** your REINFORCE implementation with a moving-average baseline: subtract `b = 0.9·b + 0.1·mean(G_t)` from each return before the backward pass. Train two agents (with and without baseline) for 500 episodes each, printing the mean reward over the last 50 episodes and the mean gradient norm per epoch. Report whether the baseline version converges faster and exhibits lower gradient norm variance.

4. **Compare** the effect of discount factor by sweeping `γ ∈ {0.90, 0.95, 0.99, 1.0}`. For each value, train REINFORCE for 300 episodes and print (a) the final 50-episode mean reward, (b) the average per-step `G_t` magnitude, and (c) the number of episodes needed to first exceed a reward of 100. Identify which `γ` gives the fastest stable convergence and explain your observation in a printed summary block.

5. **Build** a simulated multi-step GTM outreach environment and train a REINFORCE agent on it. Define an MDP where each prospect (loaded from `signals/examples/prospects_sample.csv` — a CSV with columns `industry, company_size, title, prior_touchpoints`) enters as the initial state. At each step the agent chooses an action from `{email, linkedin, call, wait}`. The environment assigns a stochastic reward at the terminal step based on prospect fit and action history (e.g., cold prospects who receive 3+ touches convert with low probability; warm titles with `company_size < 50` respond to email). Implement the full REINFORCE loop and save the trained agent and a report of the learned policy's action distribution per prospect segment to `handlers/gtm_reinforce_agent.py`. Print the average conversion rate over 200 evaluation episodes.

6. **Prove** empirically and analytically why the baseline reduces gradient variance. Instrument your REINFORCE implementation to log, per episode, the raw gradient vector norm and the baseline-subtracted gradient vector norm across 1000 episodes. Compute and print the empirical variance of each. Then write a short derivation (3–5 equations) showing that subtracting any constant `b` that does not depend on `θ` leaves the estimator unbiased (`E[G_t · ∇log π] = E[(G_t - b) · ∇log π]`) while reducing variance by a factor proportional to `Var(b) / E[G_t²]`. Save the full analysis — code, plots, equations, and empirical numbers — to `outputs/skill-policy-gradient-analysis.md`.
