# Exercises — Capstone Lesson 40: Direct Preference Optimization from Scratch

## Exercises

1. **Implement** the DPO loss function from the Bradley-Terry substitution. Given four tensors — `policy_chosen_logps`, `policy_rejected_logps`, `ref_chosen_logps`, `ref_rejected_logps` (all shape `[batch]`) and a float `beta` — compute the DPO loss as the negative log-sigmoid of `(policy_chosen - ref_chosen) - (policy_rejected - ref_rejected)` scaled by `beta`. Feed in a synthetic batch of 8 samples with known values and print the loss. Then flip one chosen/rejected pair and confirm the loss increases.

2. **Compute** sequence-level log-probabilities under a small pretrained model (e.g., `distilgpt2`) with prompt-token masking applied. Construct a prompt `"Customer: I need a demo."` followed by a chosen response `"\nAgent: Happy to set that up!"` Tokenize the full sequence, build a mask that zeroes out prompt-token positions in the loss sum, and compute the summed log-probability of only the response tokens. Print the response-level log-prob. Repeat for a rejected response `"\nAgent: Buy now."` and confirm the chosen response has a higher (or comparable) log-prob under the base model.

3. **Build** a single DPO training step end-to-end. Load two copies of a small causal LM — one frozen as `ref_model`, one trainable as `policy_model`. Create 16 synthetic `(prompt, chosen, rejected)` triples. For each triple, compute masked response log-probs under both models, feed them into your Exercise 1 loss function, call `.backward()`, and run one optimizer step. After the update, re-compute `policy_chosen_logps` and `policy_rejected_logps` and print the mean margin `chosen - rejected` before and after the step to verify it increased.

4. **Apply** DPO to GTM email data. Export a CSV of sent cold emails from your CRM (or construct one with columns `subject`, `body`, `replied` boolean, `company` from Apollo or manual enrichment). For each company that received multiple emails where exactly one was replied to, form a `(prompt, chosen, rejected)` triple where the prompt is `"Company: {company}\nEmail:"` and the responses are the subject+body of the replied vs non-replied email. Run 50 DPO steps on these triples using a small LM. Print the chosen-vs-rejected log-prob margin at steps 0, 25, and 50, and report whether the margin monotonically increases.

5. **Extend** your DPO implementation into a reusable training pipeline that loads preference data from a JSONL file, supports configurable `beta`, batch size, learning rate, and number of epochs, and writes a checkpoint plus a training-summary artifact. Run the pipeline on a GTM dataset (email replies, sales-call transcripts ranked by outcome, or Clay-enriched prospect messages ranked by meeting-booked labels). Log per-epoch mean reward margin and loss to `outputs/skill-dpo-gtm.md`. Include a short comparison section in the markdown: state one concrete advantage DPO holds over a two-stage reward-model-plus-PPO pipeline for this use case, referencing the closed-form KL-constrained solution.
