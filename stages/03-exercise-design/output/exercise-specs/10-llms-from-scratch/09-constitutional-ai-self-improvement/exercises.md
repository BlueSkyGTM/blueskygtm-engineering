# Exercises — Constitutional AI and Self-Improvement

## Exercises

1. **Implement a single-pass generate-critique-revise loop.** Pick one prompt and one constitutional principle (e.g., "The response must not state a specific number unless that number appears in the source material"). Have your model generate a response, critique it against the principle, then produce a revised response. Print the original response, the critique, and the revision to the terminal. Verify the revision differs from the original and that the critique cites a specific span of text.

2. **Write three testable constitutional principles for outbound email.** Each principle must be checkable with a yes/no predicate — not aspirational. For each principle, write (a) the principle text and (b) the automated test that would fail it. Example shape: "Principle: subject line under 50 chars. Test: `len(subject) <= 50`." Run three sample email drafts through all three principles and print pass/fail per principle. Rerun and confirm the verdicts are deterministic.

3. **Construct three deliberately broken critique cascades and fix each.** Build one cascade that produces tautological critique (the model restates the principle without flagging a violation), one that exhibits sycophancy (the model rubber-stamps the response regardless of content), and one where two principles collide (e.g., "be concise" vs. "include every required legal disclaimer"). For each, print the bad critique, diagnose which failure mode it is, then modify the prompt cascade to break the failure. Print before and after.

4. **Build a convergence tracker.** Run generate-critique-revise for 5 passes on the same prompt with a 4-principle constitution. After each pass, score the response using a *separate* scorer prompt (not the reviser itself). Print the score trajectory per pass. Then author 10 held-out human labels as ground truth and report whether your scorer agrees with the human direction of change between pass 1 and pass 5. Print agreement percentage and any principled disagreements.

5. **Configure a multi-principle constitution for a GTM content pipeline.** Target domain: LinkedIn posts generated from raw sales-call transcripts. Define at least 5 ordered principles covering (a) factual accuracy — no fabricated metrics, (b) customer pain-point specificity, (c) brand voice, (d) length, (e) call-to-action clarity. Pull 5 sample transcripts from a Clay table, Apollo export, or enrichment API output (use synthetic data if you lack live credentials). Run each transcript through generate-critique-revise and write the final revised posts plus per-pass scores and the principle that triggered the most revisions to `outputs/skill-constitutional-pipeline.md`.

6. **Design a convergence-vs-circling experiment.** Choose a domain (GTM content, support replies, or code-review comments). Define a 5+ principle constitution. Run 20 prompts through 8 revision passes each. Compute (a) mean score per pass, (b) fraction of outputs that reverse direction at least once (improve then regress), (c) the principle most often responsible for reversals. Write a one-page report to `outputs/skill-convergence-report.md` containing the per-pass score table, your verdict on whether the constitution converges or circles, and one proposed principle-reordering change with predicted impact.
