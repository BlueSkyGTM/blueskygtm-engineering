## Exercises

1. **Implement** a minimal autoregressive sampler for a 1D discrete distribution. Discretize the interval `[-4, 4]` into 64 bins, fit the conditional probabilities `p(x_i | x_{<i})` from a mixture of two Gaussians (means at -2 and 2, σ=0.5), and draw 1000 samples by sequentially sampling each bin conditioned on the previous ones. Print a text histogram (using `#` characters) of your samples alongside the analytic target distribution. The two histograms should visibly overlap.

2. **Implement** a 1D normalizing flow sampler. Define a simple invertible transformation `f(z) = a·z + b·tanh(z)` with a known (numerical) inverse, draw `z ~ N(0, 1)`, apply `f`, and verify the output distribution matches a target bimodal distribution by printing summary statistics (mean, variance, skew, kurtosis) for both the transformed samples and 1000 samples drawn directly from the target. Adjust `a` and `b` until the moments align within 10%.

3. **Build** a minimal GAN and a minimal VAE, each targeting the same bimodal 1D distribution (means at -3 and 3, σ=0.8). Use single-hidden-layer neural networks (PyTorch or pure NumPy). Train each for 2000 steps, then print: (a) the mean and std of 500 generated samples from each model, (b) the count of samples falling within `[-4, -2]` versus `[2, 4]`. **Diagnose** from the printed numbers alone which model exhibits mode collapse and which exhibits posterior collapse — state your reasoning in a printed verdict line.

4. **Trace** a forward and reverse diffusion pass through a linear noise schedule on a 1D data point. Start with `x₀ = 2.0`, use `T = 50` steps with `β_t` linearly spaced from 0.001 to 0.05. Print every 5th timestep showing: `t`, `β_t`, `α_t`, `ᾱ_t`, the forward state `x_t`, and the current SNR. Then run reverse diffusion from pure noise `x_T` using the analytic posterior (since the target is known) and print every 5th reconstructed `x_t`. Verify that the final `x̂₀` lands within 0.5 of the original `x₀ = 2.0`.

5. **Build** a handler that generates synthetic Apollo-style lead enrichment records using two different generative approaches — an autoregressive sampler for structured fields (company size bucket, revenue band, ICP fit score) and a VAE for the continuous engagement-score distribution — then writes both sample sets to `outputs/` as JSONL. The handler should accept a seed company count, sample N records from each approach, and print a side-by-side comparison of which fields each model captures well versus poorly. Artifact: `handlers/synthetic_lead_generator.py`. Run it with `python handlers/synthetic_lead_generator.py --n 200` and confirm it writes two files to `outputs/` and prints the comparison table.

6. **Design** a reference mapping document that assigns each of the five generative families to the GTM data modality where it dominates and argues why the mechanism fits. Cover at least: cold email copy, ICP firmographic distributions, sales-call transcript continuation, logo/image generation for landing pages, and time-series pipeline forecasting. For each family, cite the specific mathematical property (exact likelihood, lower-bound density, adversarial sharpness, invertible density, iterative refinement) that makes it suited or ill-suited. Artifact: `outputs/skill-generative-gtm-mapping.md`. The document must include a decision table and at least one paragraph per family per modality pairing.
