# Exercises — Bayes' Theorem

## Exercises

1. **Compute a single Bayesian posterior for an intent signal.** Given a prior P(sales-ready) = 0.04, a likelihood P(pricing-page-visit | sales-ready) = 0.78, and an evidence P(pricing-page-visit) = 0.12, compute the posterior P(sales-ready | pricing-page-visit). Write a script that prints the prior, likelihood, evidence, and posterior to four decimal places. Then change the prior to 0.15 and re-run — observe how the same signal produces a very different posterior under a different prior.

2. **Compare evidence strength across two signals.** Compute the posterior P(sales-ready | signal) independently for two signals using the same prior of 0.04: (a) company has >500 employees, with likelihood 0.35 and evidence 0.09, and (b) lead visited the competitor comparison page, with likelihood 0.62 and evidence 0.18. Print both posteriors. Then compute the Bayes factor (likelihood ratio) for each signal and print which signal carries more evidential weight, even if its posterior is lower.

3. **Implement sequential Bayesian updating for chained intent signals.** Starting with a base rate of 3%, apply four signals in order: pricing-page visit (likelihood 0.80, evidence 0.14), demo-request form fill (likelihood 0.45, evidence 0.06), >500 employees (likelihood 0.35, evidence 0.09), and return visitor within 7 days (likelihood 0.55, evidence 0.20). The posterior of each update becomes the prior of the next. Print a table showing the signal name, the prior going into that step, and the resulting posterior. Confirm that the order of signals does not change the final posterior by re-running with the signals reversed.

4. **Build a Naive Bayes classifier from scratch on firmographic and intent features.** You are given a CSV at `data/leads_train.csv` with columns: `company_size_band` (categorical: 1-10, 11-50, 51-200, 201-500, 500+), `industry` (categorical: SaaS, FinTech, HealthTech, Other), `pricing_page_visit` (0/1), `demo_requested` (0/1), `competitor_comparison_visit` (0/1), and `converted` (0/1). Implement the classifier without sklearn: compute class priors and conditional probabilities directly from frequency counts with Laplace smoothing. Load `data/leads_test.csv`, predict a probability of conversion for each row, and print the predicted probability alongside the true label for the first 15 test rows.

5. **Evaluate your lead-scoring classifier with precision, recall, and calibration.** Using the Naive Bayes classifier from Exercise 4 and the full test set, sweep a decision threshold from 0.05 to 0.95 in increments of 0.05. At each threshold, compute precision, recall, and F1. Print the full threshold table and identify the threshold that maximizes F1. Then bucket the predicted probabilities into 5 equal-width bins (0.0–0.2, 0.2–0.4, etc.) and print the average predicted probability vs. the actual conversion rate in each bin. Write a one-paragraph calibration assessment to `outputs/skill-lead-scoring.md` noting which probability ranges over- or under-predict and recommending a threshold for a SDR team that prioritizes precision over recall.
